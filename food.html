Cultural & Traditional Food

Filipino food reflects the country’s rich culture, history, and love for sharing meals with family. Each dish tells a story of heritage and togetherness — from savory classics to sweet desserts.

Main Dishes – The Heart of Every Filipino Meal

Main dishes in Filipino cuisine are rich and comforting, often served during family gatherings or fiestas. They show how Filipinos turn simple ingredients into flavorful meals filled with love and tradition.

Adobo

Made by marinating chicken or pork in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and bay leaves, then simmered until tender. A national favorite known for its tangy-salty flavor.

Sinigang

A sour soup made with tamarind and vegetables, cooked with pork, shrimp, or fish. Comforting and best during rainy days.

Kare-Kare

A peanut-based stew made with oxtail and vegetables, usually served with bagoong for extra flavor.

Lechon

Whole roasted pig cooked until the skin is crispy. A centerpiece in fiestas, symbolizing unity and celebration.

Rice & Native Delicacies – A Taste of Filipino Heritage

Rice is the heart of Filipino meals. These delicacies are enjoyed during holidays and celebrations, showing creativity and family traditions.

Bibingka

A soft rice cake baked in banana leaves, topped with salted egg and cheese, usually eaten during Christmas.

Puto

Steamed rice cake that’s light and fluffy, often paired with dinuguan or enjoyed as a snack.

Kakanin

Colorful rice-based sweets like suman, sapin-sapin, and biko — symbols of creativity and togetherness.

Arroz Caldo

A comforting rice porridge with chicken and ginger, perfect for cold days and when feeling unwell.

Desserts – Sweet Endings to Every Celebration

Filipino desserts are colorful, creamy, and full of tropical flavors — the perfect ending to every meal and occasion.

Leche Flan

Rich and creamy caramel custard made from eggs and milk. A favorite in every Filipino celebration.

Halo-Halo

Cold dessert made with shaved ice, milk, and sweet fruits. A mix of colors and flavors — just like Filipino culture.

Ube Halaya

Sweet purple yam dessert cooked with condensed milk and butter — creamy and vibrant.

Buko Pandan

Made from young coconut, pandan jelly, and sweet cream. Served cold and loved in Filipino gatherings.
